<p><p>Medical casting and splinting are non-invasive methods used to treat bone fractures, injuries, and certain medical conditions. Casting involves using a hard and rigid material, such as plaster or fiberglass, to immobilize and protect the injured area. Splinting, on the other hand, uses a softer and more flexible material to provide support and stability. Both techniques help in promoting healing, reducing pain, and preventing further damage. The choice between casting and splinting depends on the type and severity of the injury or condition.</p></p>
