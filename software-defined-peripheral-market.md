<p><p>The software-defined peripheral market consists of two main types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions. Cloud-based software-defined peripherals are hosted on remote servers and accessed over the internet, offering scalability and flexibility. On the other hand, on-premises software-defined peripherals are installed and operated locally on the user's own hardware, providing more control and security. Both types have their respective advantages and considerations, depending on factors such as data sensitivity, resource availability, and budget constraints.</p></p>
