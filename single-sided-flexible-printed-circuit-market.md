Aluminum-based circuits are lightweight and cost-effective, making them suitable for applications where weight is a concern. On the other hand, copper-based circuits offer higher conductivity and better signal transmission, making them ideal for high-frequency applications. Both types of circuits offer flexibility and durability, providing efficient solutions for a wide range of industries such as consumer electronics, automotive, and medical devices.</p></p>
