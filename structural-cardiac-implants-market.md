<p><p>Structural cardiac implants are devices used in the treatment of structural heart diseases such as heart valve defects, atrial septal defects, and ventricular septal defects. These implants are designed to either repair or replace damaged or diseased heart structures, helping to improve cardiac function and overall health.</p><p>The global structural cardiac implants market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 3.30% during the forecast period. The market growth is primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, the rise in the geriatric population, and advancements in technology leading to the development of innovative implantable devices. Additionally, the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures and the expansion of healthcare infrastructure in developing countries are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the structural cardiac implants market is the increasing adoption of transcatheter valve replacement procedures, which offer less invasive alternatives to traditional open-heart surgeries. This trend is expected to drive the demand for transcatheter heart valves and other innovative structural implants in the coming years. Other key trends include the development of bioresorbable implants, remote monitoring capabilities, and 3D printing technology for personalized implants.</p></p>
