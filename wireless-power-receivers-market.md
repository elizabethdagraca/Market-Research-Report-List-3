<p><ul><li>Inductive Coupling Wireless Power Receivers</li><li>Capacitive Coupling Wireless Power Receivers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wireless power receivers can be categorized into two main types: inductive coupling and capacitive coupling. Inductive coupling wireless power receivers use electromagnetic fields to transfer energy between the transmitter and receiver coils. This technology is commonly found in devices like smartphones and electric toothbrushes. On the other hand, capacitive coupling wireless power receivers function by transferring power through electric fields between the transmitter and receiver plates. This technology is often used in low-power applications such as wearable devices and medical implants.</p></p>
