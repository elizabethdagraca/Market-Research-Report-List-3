<p><ul><li>Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP)</li><li>Bi-Level Positive Airway Pressure (BiPAP)</li><li>Automatic Airway Pressure (APAP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sleep apnea machines, also known as positive airway pressure devices, come in three main types: Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP), Bi-Level Positive Airway Pressure (BiPAP), and Automatic Airway Pressure (APAP). </p><p>CPAP machines deliver a constant level of air pressure to keep airways open during sleep. BiPAP machines provide varying levels of pressure for inhalation and exhalation, making it easier for individuals with breathing difficulties. APAP machines automatically adjust pressure levels based on the individual's breathing patterns throughout the night. Each type of machine caters to different needs and preferences of sleep apnea patients.</p></p>
