<p><p>Active Protection Systems (APS) are designed to detect, intercept, and neutralize incoming threats to military vehicles. There are two main types of APS on the market: hard-kill and soft-kill systems. </p><p>Hard-kill systems physically intercept and destroy threats using projectiles or explosives, providing a direct defense mechanism against enemy munitions. </p><p>Soft-kill systems use electronic countermeasures like jamming or decoys to confuse or deter incoming threats without directly destroying them, offering a more passive form of defense. Both types play a crucial role in enhancing the survivability of military vehicles in combat situations.</p></p>
