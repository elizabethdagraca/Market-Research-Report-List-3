<p><p>MicroRNA sequencing (miRNA-Seq) is a process used for analyzing small RNA molecules called microRNAs. There are several types of miRNA-Seq technologies, including Sequencing by Synthesis, Ion Semiconductor Sequencing, SOLiD, and Nanopore Sequencing. Sequencing by Synthesis involves detecting fluorescent signals as nucleotides are added to a growing DNA strand. Ion Semiconductor Sequencing uses pH changes to detect nucleotide incorporation. SOLiD utilizes ligation-based methods for sequencing. Nanopore Sequencing passes DNA strands through a nanopore to analyze their sequence.</p></p>
