<p><p>Nutritive sweeteners, such as sugar and honey, provide calories and energy when consumed. They are commonly used in food and beverages for their ability to sweeten products while also providing a source of calories. On the other hand, non-nutritive sweeteners, like stevia and aspartame, do not provide calories when consumed. They are often used as sugar substitutes by people looking to reduce their calorie intake or manage conditions such as diabetes. Both types of sweeteners cater to different consumer needs and preferences within the high intensity natural sweetener market.</p></p>
