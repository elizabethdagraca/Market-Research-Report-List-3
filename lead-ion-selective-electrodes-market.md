<p><p>Lead ion selective electrodes are used in analytical chemistry to measure the concentration of lead ions in a solution. There are different types of lead ion selective electrodes, including Crystal Membrane and PVC Membrane electrodes. Crystal Membrane electrodes use a crystalline material to selectively filter lead ions, while PVC Membrane electrodes use a polyvinyl chloride membrane to separate lead ions. Both types of electrodes are commonly used in environmental monitoring, industrial processes, and research laboratories to accurately measure lead ion concentrations.</p></p>
