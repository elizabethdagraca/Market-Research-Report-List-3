<p><p>Anti-arrhythmic drugs are medications used to treat irregular heartbeats. These drugs come in two main forms: oral and parenteral. Oral medications are taken by mouth in the form of pills or liquid, allowing for easy self-administration at home. Parenteral medications are administered through injections or IV infusions in a clinical setting. Both types of anti-arrhythmic drugs work to regulate the electrical signals in the heart and restore normal heart rhythm, helping to prevent serious complications associated with arrhythmias.</p></p>
