<p><p>Ultrasonic sensors are devices that use sound waves with frequencies higher than the audible range of humans to detect objects and measure distances. These sensors work by emitting a sound wave and then measuring the time it takes for the wave to bounce back after hitting an object, allowing for precise distance calculations. <p><p>Ultrasonic sensors come in various types, including Ultrasonic Retro-Reflective Sensors, Ultrasonic Proximity Sensors, and Ultrasonic Through Beam Sensors. </p><p>Ultrasonic Retro-Reflective Sensors use sound waves to detect objects within a specified range by emitting and receiving signals bounced back off a reflector. </p><p>Ultrasonic Proximity Sensors detect objects in close proximity by emitting sound waves and measuring the time it takes for the waves to bounce back. </p><p>Ultrasonic Through Beam Sensors work by transmitting sound waves from a transmitter to a receiver, detecting objects that interrupt the waves.</p></p>
